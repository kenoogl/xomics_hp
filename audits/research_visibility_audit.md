# research_visibility_audit.md

## Task

Evaluate how effectively the laboratory website communicates its research.

Focus on research visibility, accessibility, and communication quality.

The goal is to determine whether visitors can quickly understand:

* what the lab researches
* why the research matters
* how to access the results
* how to collaborate or join

---

# Scope of Analysis

Evaluate the following areas:

1 Homepage communication
2 Research explanations
3 Publication visibility
4 Research artifacts
5 Collaboration signals
6 Outreach potential
7 Update signals

---

# 1 Homepage Communication

Check if the homepage clearly communicates:

* lab mission
* research domain
* main research themes

Expected elements:

Hero statement

Example structure:

"AI for Science Laboratory"

Short description:

One or two sentences explaining the research mission.

Research highlights section.

---

# 2 Research Topic Clarity

Evaluate each research topic page.

Each topic should contain:

Title
Short explanation
Visual figure or diagram
Representative publications

Flag issues:

research topics without explanation
research topics with only technical jargon

---

# 3 Publication Visibility

Check whether publications are easy to discover.

Verify:

dedicated publications page
chronological list
consistent formatting

Each publication should contain:

title
authors
venue
year
DOI or PDF

---

# 4 Research Artifact Availability

Check if research outputs are visible.

Recommended artifacts:

papers
slides
datasets
code repositories

Flag if publications exist but no links to research artifacts.

---

# 5 Collaboration Signals

Check if the site communicates opportunities for collaboration.

Indicators:

projects page
grants page
industry collaboration mention

---

# 6 Student Recruitment Visibility

Verify if prospective students can easily find:

join page
research topics
contact information

---

# 7 Update Signals

Users trust websites that appear actively maintained.

Check:

news page updates
recent publications
recent projects

Flag if the site appears inactive.

---

# 8 Research Storytelling

Check if research is explained using narrative structure.

Research pages should ideally include:

problem
approach
impact

Flag if pages only list technical terms.

---

# 9 Discoverability

Check whether the site includes:

structured headings
keywords
clear titles

This improves search engine visibility.

---

# 10 Credibility Signals

Check for elements that increase credibility:

institution affiliation
lab address
contact email
faculty profile

---

# 11 Report Output

Codex should produce a structured report including:

research communication score
strengths
weaknesses
missing components
recommended improvements

---

# Priority Levels

critical
recommended
optional

---

# Final Goal

Ensure that visitors can understand the lab's research within 30 seconds of landing on the homepage.

The website should function as a research communication platform, not only a repository.
